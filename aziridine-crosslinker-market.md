<p><strong>The Aziridine Crosslinker Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Difunctional Group</li><li>Trifunctional Group</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Aziridine Crosslinker market is categorized into difunctional and trifunctional groups based on the number of reactive sites. Difunctional aziridine crosslinkers possess two reactive groups, enabling them to create linear or branched polymer networks, enhancing properties like strength and durability. In contrast, trifunctional aziridine crosslinkers contain three reactive sites, promoting the formation of more complex three-dimensional networks. This increased connectivity results in superior mechanical and thermal properties, making trifunctional crosslinkers ideal for applications requiring heightened performance in coatings, adhesives, and composites.</p></p>
