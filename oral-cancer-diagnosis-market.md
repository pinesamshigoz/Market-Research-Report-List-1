<p><p>The oral cancer diagnosis market encompasses various imaging and diagnostic techniques. Computed Tomography (CT) scans provide detailed cross-sectional images, while Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) offers insights into soft tissue characteristics. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) helps identify metabolic activity of tumors. Ultrasonography uses sound waves for real-time imaging. Biopsy involves tissue sampling for definitive diagnosis. X-rays deliver quick visual assessments, and endoscopy allows direct examination of oral cavities. Fluorescence imaging enhances the visualization of cancerous tissues, alongside other emerging methods in the market.</p></p>
